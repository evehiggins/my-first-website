Project Diaries

Welcome to my Project Diaries section, where I document my journey throughout designing and developing the 'I am Bolus' game with Athena, Roy, Raymond, Beverly, Qingyue and Vicky.

Initial Concept and Planning

Focus on the colon | January 22 - February 5

I focused on designing games that directly support the learning objectives, starting with the colon and now moving into the esophagus to help Athena. I spent time reviewing course learning objectives from Canadian universities and colleges to better understand what students are expected to know about these organs. From there, I broke down the key concepts, such as anatomical sections and their functions, as well as mechanical versus chemical digestion, and used these as a foundation for brainstorming mini-game ideas. Each game concept was then translated into a visual asset sketch to explore how the learning could be experienced interactively.

Scripting the Gameplay

The Stomach Scenes | February 6 - February 19

After meeting as a script team (Athena, Roy, Raymond, and I) to refine and reduce the scope of our project, Roy drafted an overall treatment plan for the game. Building on that framework, I developed a detailed script and written storyboard to further define the narrative and gameplay flow. Our initial direction leaned more into a thriller tone, which the team responded positively to at the time. After I finalized the script, we revisited the concept and concerns were raised about the tone feeling too dark for the direction we ultimately wanted to take. We decided to put it to a vote, and the new Monk/Nirvana concept was selected. While it was difficult to set aside work I had invested significant time into, the shift pushed me to adapt quickly and refocus my energy on strengthening the new direction. I worked within the calm monastery atmosphere Roy established, adjusting the philosophical framing to emphasize self-growth and transformation rather than abstract mysticism. The experience still feels like a pilgrimage toward nirvana across the digestive journey, but this chapter now more clearly centers on controlled breakdown and restructuring. I was especially mindful of ensuring the themes maintained emotional depth without unintentionally misrepresenting religious concepts. This process highlighted for me how fluid collaborative work can be. It reinforced the importance of flexibility, clear communication, and resilience in group projects. Even when creative directions change, I remain committed to producing thoughtful, high-quality work and contributing constructively to the team.
